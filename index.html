Shipeng Dong, Ph.D.

Postdoctoral researcher, GEOMAR Helmholtz Centre for Ocean Research Kiel, Germany

I am a quantitative aquaculture ecology researcher integrating field observations with mechanistic modeling to address the most pressing ecological challenges in aquaculture. My work focuses on aquaculture ecology, quantitative ecology, population ecology, behavior ecology, species monitoring, and sustainable aquaculture. A common thread throughout my research is understanding how environmental variability — especially climate change and farming practices — shapes the performance of cultured species and the carrying capacity of aquaculture ecosystems. My current research focuses on:

  • Applications of Individual-based model (IBM) of aquatic species.
  • Applications of food-web model and regional ecosystem model.
  • Assessment of aquaculture carrying capacity.
  • Prediction of climate change impacts on aquaculture.
